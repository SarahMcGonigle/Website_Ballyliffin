Things to do in Ballyliffin & Inishowen

Situated on the northern coast of Ireland, you will never run out of things to do while staying with us.

Clean ocean air, endless sandy beaches, world-class golf and breathtaking scenery, all within a stone’s throw of the front door, makes Ballyliffin Hotel a special place that draws guests back time and time again.

Golf

Ballyliffin Hotel is the perfect base to enjoy a golf break as the world famous Ballyliffin Golf Club is less than 1km away. Two outstanding, contrasting links courses and 36 challenging holes make up one of the finest golf resorts to be found anywhere in the world.

Glenevin Water Fall

A short, but exceptionally rewarding walk through a wooded stream valley. The walk ends at Glenevin Waterfall which is one of Inishowen’s most spectacular natural attractions. The walk is virtually level and follows well prepared and maintained gravel paths. There are picnic areas along the route.

Hill Walks

Mamore Gap is the starting point of 1 of the best hill walks in Ireland. Your reward is an exceptional walking experience with unmatched Inishowen and Donegal scenery and Lough Swilly and the Atlantic Ocean.

Doagh Famine Village

Doagh Famine Village tells the story of Irish life from the Great Famine of the 1840s through until the present day. Our tour guides will take you on a journey showing how families and communities have lived on the edge generation after generation, adapting and surviving as the environment and society around the local area changed over the years.!

Based in our shore front centre in Moville, Inishowen, Co. Donegal we boast over 20 water and land activities providing all safety gear, wetsuits and equipment. We offer some of the best kayaking in Donegal, exploring the Wild Atlantic coastline and headland of Inishowen.

Fort Dunree

Fort Dunree is situated 7 miles outside Buncrana on the Inishowen Peninsula, housing a Military Museum, Wildlife Discovery Room, Coffee Shop, stunning walks and majestic views of Lough Swilly it is a must for every visitor to Donegal
